Drought Risk Across Maryland

Maryland’s drought risk is driven by agricultural exposure, water supply sensitivity, and the potential for prolonged soil moisture deficits. Relative risk varies by county based largely on modeled agricultural losses in the FEMA National Risk Index dataset.

Drought Hazard

Drought DRGT

In the FEMA National Risk Index, drought impacts in Maryland are modeled primarily through agricultural losses rather than building or population losses. Counties with greater agricultural exposure and higher modeled expected annual losses rank higher in relative drought risk.

Key Impacts

How drought conditions affect Maryland communities

  • Drought can reduce crop yields and increase irrigation costs, driving agricultural losses across affected counties.
  • Extended dry periods can reduce streamflow and groundwater recharge, affecting water supply reliability for residents, industry, and ecosystems.
  • Stressed soils, vegetation, and low-flow conditions can degrade habitat quality, water quality, and forest health.
  • Drier soils and vegetation can increase susceptibility to wildfire and brush fire conditions.
  • Long-duration drought can create cumulative economic impacts even when immediate structural damage is limited.

Read the full narrative text (technical detail)

Drought: Maryland’s drought impacts in the FEMA National Risk Index are modeled primarily through agriculture-related losses. Statewide drought Expected Annual Loss totals approximately $24.73M annually, with an estimated annualized event frequency of 12.75 events per year based on the 1996–2023 period of record.

The drought risk map shows the highest relative drought risk in Cecil, Kent, Queen Anne’s, and Caroline. A broad set of counties is categorized as relatively moderate, reflecting meaningful agricultural exposure and modeled annualized losses. Garrett, Allegany, and Somerset are categorized as relatively low. Baltimore City is categorized as No Expected Annual Losses, reflecting a highly urbanized jurisdiction with limited agricultural exposure in the NRI model.

Data source: FEMA National Risk Index (NRI), Maryland table. Metrics shown include Drought (DRGT_*) Expected Annual Loss totals and agriculture component, county relative risk ratings, event count, and exposure area.
